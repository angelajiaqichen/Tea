Brazilian Tea
  • Overview
  • History
  • Culture
  • Tea Types
Brazilian tea culture has its origins in the infused beverages, or chás (Portuguese pronunciation: [ʃas]), made by the indigenous cultures of the Amazon region and the Rio da Prata basin. It has evolved since the Portuguese colonial period to include imported varieties and tea-drinking customs. There is a popular belief in Brazil that Brazilians, especially the urban ones, have a greater taste for using sugar in teas than in other cultures, being unused to unsweetened drinks.
